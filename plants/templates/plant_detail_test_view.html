Chinese Chestnut

Castanea mollissima

Castanea mollissima, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China and other parts of East Asia. It is known for producing edible chestnuts and has a broad, spreading canopy with glossy, serrated leaves. The tree is highly resistant to chestnut blight, making it a valuable species for cultivation in chestnut restoration efforts. Chinese chestnuts thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, and they are grown both for their delicious nuts and as ornamental trees.

Height
Up to 30 feet
Width
Up to 15 feet
Moisture
Requires moderate moisture
Shade Tolerance
Tolerates partial shade
